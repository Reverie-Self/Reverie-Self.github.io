If I swung from the gallows high,
Would they whisper my name or let it die?
Sorry for the failure I became,
A fleeting ember in the flame.

Why can’t I hold my faults in hand,
Like shifting tides upon the sand?
I’m chasing dreams I’ll never fill,
A shadow chasing shadows still.

Oh, the sea takes all, but it gives me naught,
A heart weighed down with battles fought.
The waves crash hard, but I’ll never kneel,
Though it all somehow feels so real.

A noose could tie my story’s end,
But guilt’s a rope that doesn’t bend.
The winds, they howl, the heavens cry,
Yet here I stand, still asking why.

I’m chasing ghosts I cannot see,
A sailor lost, adrift, and free.
The truth’s a tempest, cold and still,
It cuts like steel, but it feels so real.

Oh, the sea takes all, but it gives me naught,
A heart weighed down with battles fought.
The waves crash hard, but I’ll never kneel,
Though it all somehow feels so real.

If I fall, let it be in the deep,
Where the secrets of sorrow forever sleep.
For every man must face his tide,
And find his peace on the other side.

So I chase the still, though the compass lies,
Through storms and stars and endless skies.
For a sailor’s curse is a heart unhealed,
And a fight for hope that still feels real.
